About Oosrand SOS

Our school, OOSRAND SECONDARY, is a no-fee Public School, situated in the East Rand township in Reiger Park of Gauteng's Ekurhuleni region. Its no-fee status is as a result of the compelling conditions of its service community and by extension its learner body. Conditions of high-unemployment, low-to-middle family income levels, critical mass of child-headed homes, rampant instances of substance abuse among adult and sadly, child family members are some of the socio-economic challenges faced by residents and the community at large. Transitioning from a conventional Secondary School to a School of specialisation in Entrepreneurship and Commerce, with a focus on Hospitality and Tourism; grants our learners and the greater community the skills required to generate businesses, be self-sufficient and academically equip for the working world that awaits them.

The School's History

The name of the school comes from its geographical location—the eastern part of the Reef (mining zone). The school was formed in 1967 as a school for "colored people" under the Apartheid laws. Being the only colored high school on the East Rand at the time, it serviced communities from as far as Nigel, Eden Park, Germiston (Dukathole), and Geluksdal, whose learners commuted by bus. This is the third building structure the school occupies, having started at what is now Drommedaris Primary, then relocated to an asbestos structure adjacent to this more recent structure in 1979. Mr. Arendse is the 6th principal of the school since its inception in 1967. Over its 47 years, the school successfully transitioned into a fully integrated school, in its composition of staff and learners.

It has always been a school catering to the educational needs of the less affluent, needy community that Reiger Park, with its informal settlements, is known for. Our school has a proud history of community involvement and uplifting. The school has a litany of sport achievements over its four and a half decades of existence. In the picture above, we have some of the school's old educators telling us their motto in their classroom. This is the second launch the school is hosting, and we are very excited to be a new and improved school. The school has faced many challenges over the four and a half decades of its existence and has become better than what it was.

Nelson Mandela's Visit

On the 26th of June 1995, the first black president of South Africa visited Oosrand Secondary school. Till this day, the staff of Oosrand cannot forget Nelson Mandela's visit. It was such an honor to have the president at the school. The staff and principal made a special welcome in which he received a bunch of gifts and flowers. The school praised him in many ways. He gave a heart-touching speech in which he said, "To understand your own country you must know what is going on in the rest of the world. Only then will you appreciate your own country. We have talent and we must discover and develop it. The greatest glory in life is not in falling but in rising every time you fall. Someone who can do what only a few others can do is great, but someone who can do what no other person has done before is a genius, a national asset, an institution. The greatest of all is the one who can use that knowledge to serve his country and people." Quoting Shakespeare: "A coward dies a thousand deaths while a valiant man dies but once."
